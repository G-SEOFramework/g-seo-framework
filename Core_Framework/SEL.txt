# Structural Evaluation Layer (SEL)

## Definition

The Structural Evaluation Layer (SEL) is a system-independent layer defined by the G-SEO Framework where digital content is evaluated, organized, and optimized based on structural and semantic criteria.

It exists independently of search engines, ranking systems, and generative model outputs.

---

## Function

Within the SEL, the G-SEO Framework performs:

- Structural evaluation — assessing clarity, organization, and interpretability  
- Structural organization — arranging content into coherent, consistent structures  
- Structural optimization — refining content for structural clarity and reuse  
- Scoring — calculating the G-SEO Score as a measure of structural alignment  

---

## Position

The SEL is not a system, tool, or process.

It is a defined layer where structural evaluation occurs.

Its identity is derived entirely from the activity of the G-SEO Framework.

---

## Scope

The SEL operates independently of:

- Industry  
- Content type  
- Platform  
- Audience  
- Search or generative systems  

It evaluates structure only.

It does not evaluate intent, performance, rankings, or outcomes.

---

## Purpose

The SEL provides a neutral, stable environment for preparing digital content for interpretation and reuse by generative search systems.

It ensures that content maintains clarity, coherence, and meaning when used outside its original context.
